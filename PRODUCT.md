# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Crypto traders on Sui mainnet, from curious newcomers to bot tinkerers. They arrive skeptical (web3 + trading bots = scam-dense category), use the app on desktop, and their first job is deciding whether to trust it enough to download the local agent and deposit 10 USDC. Returning users manage running bots, check PnL, and browse the skill marketplace.

## Product Purpose

Suirobo (by Team Autobots) is a self-custody AI trading bot ecosystem on Sui: users download a free local agent (.exe that holds their keys on their own machine), equip it with bot skills from a Walrus-hosted marketplace, and run automated DeepBook margin trades 24/7. Success = a new user goes download → pick skill → fund pool → start bot without help, and trusts the system enough to leave it running.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, technical, alive. Tagline: "Own your bot. Own your keys." Self-custody is the #1 selling point and is repeated at every touchpoint. The interface should feel like a precision trading terminal that happens to be friendly — not a hype site.

## Anti-references

- CEX bot landing pages (3Commas-style gradient-text hype, "10x your profits")
- Generic AI-trading template sites: gradient headlines, emoji icons, vague "Era of AI" copy
- Anything that hides fees or custody details — this product wins by being explicit

## Design Principles

1. Show, don't claim — live data (real chart, real PnL, real skill stats) beats marketing copy everywhere.
2. Self-custody first — every surface reminds the user their keys never leave their machine.
3. One accent, one meaning — Sui Blue (#4DA2FF) for actions/identity; green/red strictly reserved for profit/loss data.
4. The next step is always visible — every screen answers "what do I do now" (download → skill → fund → start).
5. Fees and risks in plain sight — 0.01 SUI per open, close free, min 10 USDC pool; stated, never buried.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

English-only UI (international audience; codebase comments may be Vietnamese). Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast on dark surfaces. Profit/loss never encoded by color alone (always signed numbers). Reduced-motion fallbacks for any animation.
